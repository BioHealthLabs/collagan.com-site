How to choose the right collagen for your goal

Different products target slightly different goals. Your age, diet, joint history and budget all matter more than brand hype.

  • Skin & hair focus: type I (bovine or marine) at 5–10 g per day.
  • Joint focus: look for type II collagen or blends that include it.
  • Budget focus: plain, unflavoured powders are almost always cheaper per gram than fancy “beauty” blends.

Who should be cautious with collagen?

Collagen is not right for everyone. A quick conversation with your doctor is wise if you are in any of these groups:

  • Fish, shellfish or beef allergies (depending on source).
  • Kidney disease or medically restricted protein intake.
  • Pregnant, planning pregnancy or breastfeeding.
  • On blood thinners or multiple long-term medications.

Collagen FAQ

How long does it take to see results from collagen?

Most clinical trials run for at least 8–12 weeks, with some going up to 6 months. Many people report changes in skin hydration and joint comfort after several months of daily use – not days.

Do I need to take collagen on an empty stomach?

There is no strong evidence that timing is critical. Most people simply take collagen at the same time each day in a drink or smoothie they already enjoy and focus on consistency over timing hacks.

Can I use collagen if I already eat a high-protein diet?

A well-planned, protein-sufficient diet is always the foundation. Collagen can add targeted amino acids like glycine and proline, but it should be seen as a complement to, not a replacement for, whole-food protein sources.
